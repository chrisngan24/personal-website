Hi there! I am a student at the University of Waterloo studying System Design Engineering. As an engineering student, I am excited to be learning a diverse variety of skills, from software development to solution architecture. My ultimate goal is to be developing solutions for problems that stretch beyond the scope of a traditional engineer and to help make the world a better place. I want to work with issues such as poverty, food security and public healthcare - but with a systematic and technical perspective. So until that dream is completely achieved, I'll keep on exploring the world!

When design is motivated by the art of creation itself, it allows for intelligence to thrive and innovation to breathe.

  • Medidata

  • Play on Time

    Play On Time is a metronome application that has been developed for Sony Smartwatch. Rather than acting as a traditional metronome that uses sound to provide a steady tempo, it uses the vibrator within the device to generate timed pulses. It comes with a simple interface that allows the user to control the tempo.

    Github

  • Electric Piano

    This was a final project for a lab course I took in my first year of Engineering at Waterloo. It uses an FPGA board to control the frequency of two speakers by simply sending a binary signal at controlled time intervals. This oscillation of high and low created the sweet tones of an electric piano.

  • Talklight

    Talk Light is an experiment developed to practice and display some techniques learned during my work experience. This is a project that is meant to display and analyze information on a room's lighting. The data is acquired by a python script and then pushed to a database. A webapp then displays the data in graphical form which can then be analyzed.

    Github

  • Myophysio

    Submission at the Next36 Wearable Hackathon. MyoPhysio is a physiotherapy tool for patients to use at home to gain better feedback of recovery exercises. Was part of the first round of public developers on Thalmic Lab's Myo.
